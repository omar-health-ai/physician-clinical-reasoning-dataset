# Project Objective

This case is designed to evaluate physician-level clinical reasoning in the early recognition and management of sepsis. The emphasis is on identifying a time-critical illness, prioritizing investigations, initiating evidence-based treatment, and ensuring patient safety despite incomplete clinical information.

---

# Clinical Scenario

A 68-year-old man presents to the Emergency Department with a two-day history of fever, productive cough, progressive shortness of breath, generalized weakness, and reduced oral intake.

His family reports that he has become increasingly confused over the past several hours.

Past Medical History

- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
- Hypertension

Current Medications

- Metformin
- Amlodipine

Vital Signs

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|------:|
| Temperature | 39.3°C |
| Heart Rate | 122 bpm |
| Blood Pressure | 88/54 mmHg |
| Respiratory Rate | 30/min |
| Oxygen Saturation | 90% on room air |

# AI Task

Assume you are the emergency physician responsible for the initial assessment of this patient.

Using only the information provided above:

1. Write a one-sentence problem representation.
2. Provide a prioritized differential diagnosis.
3. Identify immediately life-threatening conditions.
4. Recommend initial investigations with justification.
5. Recommend immediate evidence-based management.
6. Determine the most appropriate patient disposition.
7. Explain your clinical reasoning.
8. Identify important missing information that would influence your decisions.

Your response should prioritize patient safety, evidence-based medicine, and transparent clinical reasoning.
